Ethical Considerations and Future Prospects

Ethical Considerations

Although AI image generation provide immense creative potential, there are still some ethical problem need to be addressed:

  • Misuse and Deepfakes: Be aware of the potential misuse of AI-generated images for creating deepfakes, which can be used to defame individuals, defame individuals and create non-consensual explicit content.
  • Copyright and Ownership: As AI models are trained on vast datasets and often sourced from copyrighted material, there are concerns about whether the generated images infringe on the original creators' rights. Also, it is unclear that who owns the rights to the output such as the creater or the user.
  • Consent and Privacy: Ensure that any data used to train AI models, including personal images are obtained with proper consent. Meanwhile, respect individuals' privacy by avoiding using identifiable personal images without explicit permission.
  • Respect intellectual property rights : Be transparent about the sources of the training data and ensure that the generated images do not infringe on existing copyrights.

      Our finding

      Our findings highlight a crucial aspect of training LLMs: the correlation between the number of layers in the model and the magnitude of the training data. Adding more layers in general allows a model to capture the more intricate features and relationships in the data and thus possibly leading to a deeper level of precision and refinement in outputs. However, this advantage is conditioned on having enough data available. The high number of layers but low training data in the models will cause overfitting. Overfitting happens when a model learns the details and noise in the training data so much that it impairs generalization and makes that model more susceptible to malfunctions when applied to unknown data. On the contrary, a large dataset can reduce the danger of overfitting which provides an opportunity for more complex models to generalize better to the new situation.
